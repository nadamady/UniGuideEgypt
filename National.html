Zewail City of Science, Technology and Innovation

Zewail City of Science, Technology and Innovation is a nonprofit, independent institution of learning, research and innovation. It's named after its founder the late Dr. Ahmed Zewail, the Nobel laureate in chemistry 1999. After numerous delays, the project was revived by the Egyptian cabinet's decree on May 11, 2011 following the January 25 Revolution.

How are tuition fees calculated?

  • Tuition fee is calculated based on the number of registered credit hours. The tuition of each credit hour for academic year 2024/2025:
  • 3,740 EGP (Three Thousand and Four Hundred Egyptian Pounds) for Egyptian students.
  • 350 USD (Three Hundred and Fifty Dollars) for non-Egyptian students.

Note

There will be a limited number of fellowships/financial support from the research centers at Zewail City that will be available to the top applicants based on their credentials. The financial aid form will be sent to the applied applicants.

FAQ Section
In awarding financial support, the financial support committee considers:⌄
  • 1. The student's individual/family circumstances.
  • 2. The number of submitted applications for financial support.
  • 3. The university resources.
  • 4. the student's academic background.

    Galala University

    Galala University is a national, non-profit Egyptian university located in Al Galala in Suez. The university comprises 13 faculties covering various academic disciplines and was established by a presidential decree in August 2020.

    Tuition Fees Disclaimer

    The tuition fees listed are based on the previous academic year (2024/2025) and are subject to change. Updated fees for the 2025/2026 academic year will be announced soon. Applicants are encouraged to check back regularly for the latest updates.

    Refund Policy

    • Before the start of classes: Full refund with 10% deduction of the semester tuition fees for new students, plus full deduction of the admission administrative fees.
    • During the first and second week of classes: Full refund with 50% deduction of the semester tuition fees for new students, plus full deduction of the admission administrative fees.
    • Starting from the third week of classes: No refund is allowed.

    Note:

    No other fees are subject to refund.

    King Salman International University

    King Salman International University is a national (Ahleya), non-profit Egyptian university located in Sinai across three campuses in Ras Sedr, El Tor and Sharm El Sheikh. The university includes 13 faculties in different fields of study. It was established in August 2020 by a decision of Abdel Fattah El-Sisi, President of Egypt, and was named after Salman of Saudi Arabia.

    Application Process Summary

    • Applications are submitted online through the official application webpage.
    • Students choose their desired academic field/program and fill out personal and academic details.
    • Upload documents to verify submitted information. A non-refundable application fee of 1,600 EGP is required.
    • Technical diploma holders may apply for certain fields (e.g., Engineering, Tourism, Admin Sciences).
    • If initially accepted, students will receive an email and must visit campus with original documents.
    • Students must pay 50% of tuition + service & medical exam fees within 2 days of acceptance.
    • Final acceptance is conditional upon medical exam and personal interview.
    • Students cannot be enrolled in multiple universities; prior enrollment must be canceled first.
    • Registration cancellation before the academic year allows a refund minus 10% of paid fees.
    • Support is available via hotline or on-campus assistance if students cannot apply online.

    Nile University

    Nile University (NU) is the first non-profit, research and entrepreneurial university in Egypt. Founded in July 2006 with the support of the Egyptian Ministry of Communications and Information Technology. The university has undergraduate programs and graduate programs alongside its research centers. It is located in Juhayna Square on 26 July Corridor, Sheikh Zayed, Giza. It includes 5 Schools: School of Engineering and Applied Sciences, School of Information Technology and Computer Science, School of Business Administration, School of Biotechnology, Graduate School of Management of Technology.

    Scholarship Policy

    • Full Scholarships: 100% scholarship is offered to 50 students among the top 500 students of Egyptian Thanaweya Amma.
    • Full Scholarships: 100% scholarship is offered to 20 students among the top students of STEM schools.
    • Merit Scholarships: A merit scholarship is offered to academically outstanding students in the first year based on their high school score.
    • Siblings of NU students get 10% scholarship.
    • Government employees' sons and daughters get 10% scholarship.
    • 5% scholarship is granted for excellence in sports.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. When do university applications open in Egypt? ⌄
Most private universities start applications around June or July, while public university coordination (Tansik) opens after Thanaweya Amma results are released, typically in August.
2. Can I apply to multiple universities at once? ⌄
Yes, you can apply to multiple private or national universities, but each has its own application system. For public universities, you must go through the Tansik portal.
3. What certificates are accepted for admission? ⌄
  • Thanaweya Amma (Egyptian general secondary)
  • Azhar certificate
  • IGCSE/GCSE
  • American Diploma
  • IB (International Baccalaureate)
  • STEM Schools
  • Other national/international systems (with specific rules)
4. What is the minimum grade for applying to private universities? ⌄
It varies yearly and by faculty. For example, Medicine may require 85–90%, while Business or IT may accept 60–70%. Always check the latest thresholds.
5. Do universities offer scholarships or financial aid? ⌄
Yes, many private and international universities offer:
  • Academic excellence scholarships
  • Need-based discounts
  • Sibling discounts
  • Sports or special talent scholarships
6. Is the application process online or in-person? ⌄
Most private and international universities have online applications. Public universities use the Tansik e-portal.
7. Can I transfer from one university to another? ⌄
Yes, but you must meet the target university's transfer rules, including:
  • Minimum GPA
  • Curriculum compatibility
  • Number of transferable credit hours

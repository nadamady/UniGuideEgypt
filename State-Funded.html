Ain Shams University (ASU)

Ain Shams University was founded in July 1950, the third-oldest non-sectarian native public Egyptian university (ancient Islamic universities such as Al-Azhar and private institutions such as the American University in Cairo are older), under the name of Ibrahim Pasha's University. Its site used to be a former royal palace, called the Zafarana Palace. The two earlier universities of this kind are Cairo University (Fuad I university formerly) and Alexandria University (Farouk I university formerly).

Why Ain Shams University is a Great Choice

  • Long-standing academic legacy, producing many influential scientists, doctors, engineers, and leaders in Egypt and the Arab world.
  • Wide range of programs in medicine, engineering, science, arts, business, and more.
  • Renowned faculties like the Faculty of Medicine, which is considered one of the best in the country.
  • Accredited programs aligned with national and international academic standards.
  • Ranked among the top universities in Africa and the Arab region.
  • International partnerships and student exchange programs with universities in Europe, Asia, and the U.S.

Note

Ain Shams University continues to expand its academic reach and global recognition through innovation, research, and collaboration.

Al-Azhar University (AZU)

The Al-Azhar University is a public university in Cairo, Egypt. Associated with Al-Azhar Al-Sharif in Islamic Cairo, it is Egypt's oldest degree-granting university and is known as one of the most prestigious universities for Islamic learning. In addition to higher education, Al-Azhar oversees a national network of schools with approximately two million students. As of 1996, over 4,000 teaching institutes in Egypt were affiliated with the university.

How is Al-Azhar University structured?

  • Multiple branches nationwide, allowing students from various regions to access education closer to home.
  • Duplicate faculties are often available in different locations.
  • Gender-separated campuses are common, with dedicated faculties for male and female students.

Note

The availability of specific programs and faculties may vary by location.

    Assiut University (AUN)

    Assiut University is a university located in Assiut, Egypt. It was established in October 1957 as the first university in Upper Egypt. The university includes 16 faculties and three institutes.

    Why Assiut Is a Great Choice for University

    • Reputable University — Assiut University is one of Egypt's oldest and most respected universities. It excels in fields like Medicine, Engineering, Science, Agriculture, and Pharmacy, with a strong reputation across the Middle East and Africa.
    • Affordable Living — The cost of living in Assiut is significantly lower than in Cairo or Alexandria. Housing, food, and transportation are all budget-friendly, giving students more value for their money.
    • Focused Learning Environment — Smaller class sizes and fewer urban distractions help students stay academically focused. Professors are known for being accessible and supportive.
    • International Students Welcome — Assiut University hosts a growing international community, especially from Africa and Asia. Dedicated offices support international students with visas, housing, and orientation services.

    Aswan University branch was established in 1974 as a branch of Assiut University. The study started on October of the academic year 1973/1974 in the faculty of education and the first bachelor was given in 1978 then started the enrollment in master's degree in the faculty of science in the academic year 1977/1978. and then Aswan branch was affiliated to it, new faculties were established (faculty of arts - faculty of social work - faculty of engineering).

    Why Aswan Is a Great Choice for University

    • Growing Academic Reputation — Aswan University is one of the fastest-growing public universities in Egypt. It is ranked among the top 500 universities worldwide in the Times Higher Education Impact Rankings (based on the UN Sustainable Development Goals). It offers strong programs in Engineering, Energy, Medicine, and African Studies.
    • Very Low Cost of Living — Aswan is one of the most affordable cities in Egypt for students. Student dormitories and private housing are budget-friendly, and daily expenses like food and transport are low.
    • Natural Beauty + Inspiration — Surrounded by the Nile River, desert landscapes, and ancient historical sites like Philae Temple and Abu Simbel, Aswan offers a peaceful, inspiring environment for students who appreciate nature and history.

    Note

    Aswan University provides an enriching academic experience in one of Egypt’s most scenic and affordable cities—ideal for focused study and cultural exploration.

    Benha University (BU)

    Benha University is an Egyptian government university in the city of Benha, the capital of Al Qalyubiyah Governorate. It was established according to a decree on 25 November 1976 as a branch from Zagazig University in Benha, with the faculties of Commerce, Education, Agriculture of Moshtohor, Engineering of Shobra and Medicine. In 1981-1982, the faculties of Arts, Science of Benha and Veterinary Medicine of Moshtohor were founded. On 1 August 2005 it became an independent university from Zagazig University.

    Why Banha University Is a Great Choice for University

    • Strategic Location — Located in Qalyubia Governorate, just outside of Cairo, Banha University offers proximity to the capital's opportunities without the chaos and high cost of living. It’s well-connected via public transportation, making it ideal for daily commuters.
    • Growing Academic Reputation — Though established in 2005, Banha University has rapidly grown in both research and academic quality. It holds strong national rankings in fields like engineering, agriculture, veterinary medicine, science, and computer science. The university is also known for being innovation-driven, with several collaborations with international institutions.
    • Affordable & Accessible — Tuition fees and living costs are lower compared to many Cairo-based universities. Banha University also provides scholarships and support programs for high-achieving and international students.

    Note

    Banha University offers a strong mix of academic growth, affordability, and location advantage—ideal for students looking for balance and opportunity.

    Cairo University (CU)

    Cairo University is Egypt's premier public university. Its main campus is in Giza, immediately across the Nile from Cairo. It was founded on 21 December 1908; after being housed in various parts of Cairo, its faculties, beginning with the Faculty of Arts, were established on its current main campus in Giza in October 1929.

Features both traditional and advanced programs like AI, biotechnology, and nanoscience. Programs are available in Arabic and English for local and international students alike.
    • Elite Faculty & Alumni — Home to many internationally recognized professors and researchers. Alumni include Nobel laureates, government ministers, renowned scientists, writers, and diplomats. Cairo University boasts a strong global reputation and professional network.

    Note

    With a legacy of excellence, international prestige, and academic diversity, Cairo University remains one of Egypt's and the region’s most respected educational institutions.
